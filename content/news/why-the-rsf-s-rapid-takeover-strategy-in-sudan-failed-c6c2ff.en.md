title: Why the RSF’s Rapid Takeover Strategy in Sudan Failed

From the beginning of Sudan’s war, the strategy pursued by the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) and its supporting network relied on one central assumption: that the Sudanese military leadership could be rapidly neutralized, forcing the collapse of the military institution and allowing for a swift takeover of the state. Public statements made during the opening days of the conflict reinforced this impression. The expectation seemed to be that senior military commanders would either surrender quickly or be eliminated altogether, creating political paralysis and institutional breakdown within days.
