**World Bank · 2015 · 226 pages**

The most important economic document in the Kandaka library. Written at the moment Sudan hit rock bottom — 75% of fiscal revenue gone with South Sudan, the Gezira Scheme in collapse, gold and gum arabic the only remaining export earners — it is the most comprehensive and actionable analysis of Sudan's economic predicament and potential.

## Core Finding

A 2% annual increase in crop agriculture productivity would:
- Raise GDP growth by ~1 percentage point per year
- Reduce the poverty rate from 38.4% (baseline) to 29.4% by 2030
- Lift millions of Sudanese out of poverty from a single sector intervention

## The Three-Stage Ladder

The CEM maps a sequenced development pathway specific to Sudan's endowments:

1. **Revive agriculture** — Sudan's only sector with genuine comparative advantage post-oil. Gezira Scheme rehabilitation, fertiliser access (currently 7.3 kg/ha vs Ethiopia's 17 kg/ha), exchange rate correction (overvalued for 40 years, penalising exporters).

2. **Build agro-processing** — Capture more value from sesame, gum arabic, sorghum, and livestock rather than exporting raw. Sudan supplies ~80% of the world's gum arabic; it processes almost none.

3. **Scale into light manufacturing** — Using the agro-processing platform as a foundation for broader productive diversification.

## Key Constraints Identified

- Real exchange rate overvalued for four decades, making Sudanese agricultural exports uncompetitive
- Fertiliser use at 7.3 kg/ha (one of the lowest in Africa)
- Near-total collapse of the Gezira Scheme, once 900,000 hectares of irrigated farmland
- Dependence on gold and gum arabic as only remaining foreign exchange earners post-2011
- Fiscal space destroyed by loss of oil revenues

## Why It Remains Relevant in 2026

The 2025 World Bank Sudan Economic Update confirmed that the 2023 war has dramatically set back Sudan's economy — but it did not change the underlying diagnosis. The agricultural endowments remain. The potential remains. The CEM describes what Sudan needs to rebuild *toward*; the 2025 update describes how much further back the starting line has moved.

This paper reads best alongside the [World Bank Sudan Economic Update 2025](/development/world-bank-sudan-economic-update-2025/), which documents how much the 2023 war has deepened the crisis the CEM was trying to address.
